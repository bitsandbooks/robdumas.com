Curriculum Vitae

cur·ric·u·lum vi·tae /kəˈrik(y)ələm ˈvītē/ (pl. curricula vitae /-lə/) n. :a brief account of a person’s education, qualifications, and previous occupations, typically sent with a job application.

Source: Oxford Dictionary.

I'm a reference librarian working for Chicago Public Library at the Carter G. Woodson Regional Library. I provide reference service, order new books, weed out old ones, teach computer classes, plan and host library programs and supervise our library pages. I enjoy my work, especially when someone walks out happier than when they walked in. It's a great feeling.

In addition to having a Master's degree in Information & Library Science from Pratt Institute, I also have a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science from New York Institute of Technology. I am equally comfortable in a variety of computer operating systems, such as Windows, Mac OS X and Linux. Furthermore, I am also a web and application developer, specifically using HTML, CSS, PHP, C, C++, Objective-C, Python, SQL and more. I'm of the opinion that smart technology, well-designed and properly applied, can revolutionize librarianship and make us more relevant than ever.

I am also an occasional speaker at library technology events. Most recently, I gave a “lightning talk” entitled Git in 5 Minutes at the Code4Lib conference in February 2013. You can read my presentations and other projects on the Projects page.
